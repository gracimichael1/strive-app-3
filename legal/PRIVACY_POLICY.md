## 1. Information We Collect

### 1.1 Personal Information You Provide

When you create an account or use the Service, we may collect:

- **Account Information**: Name, email address, and password
- **Athlete Profile Information**: Date of birth, gender, gymnastics level (e.g., USAG Levels 1–10, Xcel), gym name, and coach name
- **Subscription Information**: Subscription tier selection (Free, Competitive, Elite, Coach, or Gym)

### 1.2 Video Data

- **Gymnastic Routine Videos**: Videos you upload for AI-powered analysis. These videos may contain images of the athlete performing gymnastics routines.

### 1.3 Analysis and Usage Data

- **Analysis Results**: Scores, deductions, skill assessments, and training recommendations generated by the Service
- **Analysis History**: A record of past analyses and score progression over time
- **App Interactions**: Features used, pages viewed, and actions taken within the Service

### 1.4 Device and Technical Data

- **Browser and Device Information**: Browser type, operating system, device type, and screen resolution
- **Log Data**: IP address, access times, and referring URLs
- **Performance Data**: App load times, errors, and crash reports

### 1.5 Payment Data

- **Payment Processing**: Payment transactions are processed by Stripe, our third-party payment processor. We do **not** store, collect, or have access to your full credit card number, debit card number, or bank account information. We may receive limited transaction information from Stripe, such as the last four digits of your card, card brand, and billing address, for record-keeping and customer support purposes.

## 4. Children's Privacy (COPPA)

**This section is critically important.** Strive is a gymnastics analysis platform used by athletes of all ages, including children under 13. We are committed to complying with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act ("COPPA") and take the following measures to protect children's privacy.

### 4.1 Parental Consent Required

We require **verifiable parental consent** before collecting any personal information from a child under 13 years of age. When a user indicates they are under 13 during account registration, we will:

1. Pause the registration process and collect only the parent or guardian's email address
2. Send the parent or guardian a consent form explaining what information we collect, how it is used, and how it is shared
3. Require the parent or guardian to provide verifiable consent (e.g., by signing an electronic consent form, providing a credit card transaction, or another COPPA-approved verification method) before allowing the child to use the Service
4. Not collect any additional personal information from the child until parental consent is obtained

### 4.2 Separate Consent for Video Sharing

Because gymnastic routine videos are sent to Google's Generative AI API for analysis, we require **separate, specific parental consent** for the transmission of a child's video data to this third-party service. This consent is in addition to the general parental consent for account creation.

### 4.3 Information Collected from Children

When parental consent is obtained, we collect only the information reasonably necessary to provide the Service:

- Child's first name (last name is optional)
- Date of birth (to verify age and determine gymnastics level)
- Gender (required for level-appropriate scoring)
- Gymnastics level
- Video recordings of gymnastic routines (uploaded by the child or their parent/coach)

### 4.4 What We Do NOT Do with Children's Data

- We do **not** sell children's personal information to any third party
- We do **not** use children's personal information for behavioral advertising or targeted marketing
- We do **not** share children's data with third parties except as necessary to provide the Service (as described in Section 3)
- We do **not** condition a child's participation on the collection of more personal information than is reasonably necessary

### 4.5 Parental Rights

Parents and legal guardians have the right to:

- **Review** the personal information we have collected from their child
- **Request deletion** of their child's personal information
- **Refuse further collection** of their child's personal information (which may result in the inability to use certain features of the Service)
- **Withdraw consent** at any time by contacting us at the address provided in Section 11

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at **privacy@striveapp.com** with the subject line "COPPA Request." We will verify your identity as the child's parent or guardian before processing any request.

### 4.6 Schools and Coaches

If a coach or gym creates accounts on behalf of minor athletes, the coach or gym represents that they have obtained the necessary parental consent before creating accounts for children under 13.

## 5. Biometric Data Notice (BIPA Compliance)

This section provides notice in accordance with the Illinois Biometric Information Privacy Act ("BIPA"), 740 ILCS 14/1, et seq., and similar state biometric privacy laws.

### 5.1 What Biometric Data We Collect

Our Service includes a pose detection feature that analyzes video frames to extract **body geometry data**, including joint positions, skeletal landmarks, body angles, and movement trajectories. This data constitutes **biometric information** as defined under BIPA and similar state laws.

### 5.2 Purpose of Collection

Biometric data is collected and used solely for the purpose of:

- Analyzing gymnastic form, body positioning, and technique
- Generating deduction assessments and skill scores
- Providing visual overlays and feedback to improve athletic performance

### 5.3 Consent

We obtain **informed written consent** (or electronic equivalent) before collecting biometric data. This consent is obtained:

- During the account registration process for all users
- Through the separate parental consent process for users under 13 (see Section 4)
- Before the first video analysis that involves pose detection

You may withdraw your consent at any time by contacting us at **privacy@striveapp.com**.

### 5.4 Retention and Destruction

- Biometric data derived from pose detection is **deleted within three (3) years** of the individual's last interaction with the Service, or when the initial purpose for collecting the data has been satisfied, whichever occurs first.
- When an account is deleted, associated biometric data is permanently destroyed within 30 days.
- We maintain a publicly available retention schedule (this policy serves as that schedule).

### 5.5 Disclosure and Sale Prohibition

- We do **not** sell, lease, trade, or otherwise profit from any user's biometric data.
- We do **not** disclose biometric data to any third party except:
  - With the user's (or parent/guardian's) explicit consent, or
  - As required by law, subpoena, or court order

### 5.6 Security of Biometric Data

Biometric data is protected using a standard of care equal to or more protective than the measures we use for other confidential and sensitive information. Specific measures include:

- Encryption in transit and at rest
- Access controls limiting biometric data access to authorized systems and personnel
- Regular security assessments

## 8. Data Security

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your personal information:

### 8.1 Technical Safeguards

- **Encryption in Transit**: All data transmitted between your device and our servers is encrypted using HTTPS/TLS
- **API Key Security**: API keys and secrets are stored server-side in environment variables and are never exposed to client-side code
- **Access Controls**: CORS (Cross-Origin Resource Sharing) policies restrict API access to authorized domains only
- **Content Security Policy**: CSP headers are implemented to prevent cross-site scripting and other injection attacks
- **Secure Hosting**: Our application is hosted on Vercel, which maintains SOC 2 Type II compliance

### 8.2 Organizational Safeguards

- Access to personal information is limited to authorized personnel on a need-to-know basis
- We conduct regular reviews of our data collection, storage, and processing practices
- We evaluate the security practices of our third-party service providers

### 8.3 Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach that compromises your personal information, we will:

- Notify affected users within **72 hours** of discovering the breach (or as required by applicable law)
- Provide details about what information was affected and what steps we are taking
- Notify relevant regulatory authorities as required by law

### 8.4 Limitations

While we strive to protect your personal information, no method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security, but we are committed to maintaining commercially reasonable safeguards.
